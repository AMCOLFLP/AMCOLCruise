Applied Activity and Performance Task

Create a practical cruise-operations output using the unit concepts, professional English, operational data, and decision tool.

Assignment Brief

Create a complete complaint-resolution plan and a plain-language public announcement for a venue-access change. Include accessibility support, timing, ownership, and follow-up.

Required Procedure

  1. Read the operational brief and list confirmed facts.
  2. Identify guest needs, service requirements, safety concerns, and missing information.
  3. Assign department ownership and escalation level.
  4. Use the decision tool below to test workload, timing, priority, capacity, or accountability.
  5. Prepare the guest-facing message.
  6. Prepare the crew operational report or handover.
  7. Review your work for exact names, cabin/deck/location, time, numbers, and follow-up.

Guest-Concern Priority Guide

This is a classroom decision aid. Actual ship procedures and company systems take priority.

Structured Analysis Template

Professional Communication Output

Write a guest-facing response and an internal service-recovery record. The guest-facing message must show empathy without making an unverified claim.

Performance Rubric

Operational Accuracy — 25%

Correct facts, sequence, department, and procedure.

Professional English — 25%

Clear grammar, precise vocabulary, suitable tone, and concise structure.

Decision Quality — 25%

Priority, safety, service, timing, and realistic action.

Completion & Follow-Up — 25%

Documentation, ownership, confirmation, and prevention.

STEP-BY-STEP TASK SUPPORT

Spoon-Fed Performance Task Guide

The activity should be completed as a controlled professional task, not as an unsupported creative exercise. Students may use the following sequence as a checklist while preparing their work.

Before You Start

  1. Read the task once to understand the general situation.
  2. Read it again and mark people, location, time, problem, risk, and required output.
  3. Review the relevant vocabulary and choose at least five terms that fit naturally.
  4. Decide whether the language is guest-facing, crew operational, supervisor-level, or safety-critical.
  5. Check the rubric before writing so every scoring requirement appears in the final output.

Planning Template

Planning QuestionStudent Notes
What exactly happened?Use one factual sentence.
Who is affected?Name the guest, group, crew member, or department.
Where and when?Give exact location and time.
What is the immediate priority?State safety, service, security, medical, technical, or schedule priority.
Who owns the next action?Name the department or authority.
What must be communicated?Choose guest wording and operational wording.
How will completion be checked?Give status, deadline, follow-up, or evidence.

Worked Example

Practice situation: A guest using a wheelchair reports that an accessible excursion arrangement was not available and that two previous calls were not returned. The guest is angry and also mentions feeling dizzy. The crew member must separate the complaint, accessibility support, and possible medical need while maintaining one coordinated response.

Step 1 — Priority: Identify the most immediate risk or service impact.

Step 2 — Referral: Name the correct operational owner and any supporting department.

Step 3 — Message: Use the report formula with exact location, condition, action, and time.

Sample professional output:
“Guest contacted Guest Services at 1740 regarding unavailable accessible excursion transport and two unanswered follow-up calls. Guest also reported dizziness; medical team contacted immediately with consent. Accessibility coordinator and shore-excursion manager notified. Duty manager to provide complaint update by 1815.”

Step 4 — Follow-up: State who receives the next update and what must be checked before closure.

Submission Checklist

  • My facts are clear and do not include unsupported assumptions.
  • I used the exact location, number, time, condition, or deadline.
  • I identified the correct department and responsible role.
  • I used at least five relevant vocabulary terms correctly.
  • My guest-facing language is polite and my operational language is concise.
  • I included action already taken, next action, ownership, and follow-up.
  • I checked grammar, spelling, capitalization, and professional tone.

Peer Feedback Sentence Frames

  • “The clearest operational detail is ________ because ________.”
  • “One important fact that is still missing is ________.”
  • “The responsible department should be ________ because ________.”
  • “The guest-facing sentence could sound more professional by changing ________ to ________.”
  • “The case should not be closed until ________.”
